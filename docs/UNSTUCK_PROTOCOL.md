# The Unstuck Protocol: your safety net (use it the whole way)

> The point: between Claude Code and this protocol, you can figure out ~99% of what you hit, yourself, fast. Don't overthink it, just do it. Escalate to a human (your founder / whoever set this up) only if you've genuinely tried and can't sort it out.

## When you're stuck, in order
1. **Ask Claude Code, in caveman mode.** Tell it: *"Treat me like a caveman. Explain like I'm 5. Give me every click, assume I know nothing."* You'll get zero-gap, step-by-step instructions. This alone solves most things.
2. **Screenshot + ask an AI.** Screenshot your screen and paste it into Gemini or ChatGPT with: *"I'm trying to [goal]. I expected [X], but [Y] happened. Tell me exactly how to fix it, step by step."* For live, click-by-click help, share your screen with Gemini / AI Studio Live and talk it through.
3. **Look it up on YouTube, and stay current.** This space changes fast: a how-to from a year ago can be wrong now. Copy the exact thing you're doing, search YouTube for it, and **use a video from the last 1-2 months.** Stick to the **core move**, ignore the extra rabbit-hole stuff some creators pile on; you don't need it.
4. **Go deeper with NotebookLM (optional, powerful).** Load your training docs + the relevant YouTube videos (paste the URLs straight in) into a NotebookLM notebook, then ask your question there, it answers grounded only in those sources, with citations. Keep one notebook per area (e.g. "AIOS Learning"). **NotebookLM is a learning aid, NOT your brain:** your business brain lives in your repo + Google Drive (that's what Claude reads from). Use NotebookLM to understand a topic, not to store your IP.
5. **Still stuck? Escalate.** If you've tried the above and truly can't move, ask the person who set this up (your founder / lead). Bring what you tried, where you got stuck, and a screenshot.

## The mindset
- **Don't outsource your thinking, sharpen it.** AI is a gym, not a crutch. The more you drive, the sharper you get.
- **Bias to action.** Figure it out and move; you'll be right ~99% of the time with the steps above.
- **Stick to what you SHOULD do.** It's easy to chase shiny objects ("look what I CAN do"). Ground every move in the one thing that actually matters right now.

## Copy-paste prompts that work
Don't know where to start:
> "I'm trying to **[your goal]** but I don't know the first step. Treat me like a caveman. Give me the major phases as a numbered list, then walk me through step 1 in extreme detail, every click."

Something broke:
> "I expected **[what should have happened]** but **[what actually happened]**. Here's a screenshot. Tell me why, then exactly how to fix it, step by step."

Totally overwhelmed:
> "I don't know what I should be doing. Ask me questions, then turn my answers into a short, clear action list I can run without getting stuck."

**The one-step rule:** do only the first step it gives you. Worked? Reply "Done. Next." Failed? Screenshot it and reply "This didn't work, see image." Repeat until done. If it goes too fast: "Break that last step into 3 smaller micro-steps."
