# Approval Rubric

> Mutual standards for what "done" looks like. Agency sets the quality floor. Client sets the taste.

---

## Approval Process

1. Agency delivers to `02_Deliverables/{type}/`
2. Client reviews against this rubric + `qc_preferences.md`
3. Feedback goes in `06_Communication/feedback_log.md`
4. Revisions (if needed) land back in `02_Deliverables/`
5. Final approval logged in `06_Communication/decisions_log.md`

---

## Quality Standards (Agency-Set)

These are non-negotiable quality gates. Every deliverable meets these before you see it.

| Standard | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Strategically aligned** | Matches your F6 positioning, avatar, and offer |
| **Grammatically clean** | No typos, no broken formatting |
| **Platform-ready** | Formatted for the destination (ad platform, email tool, page builder) |
| **CRO-informed** | Conversion best practices applied |

---

## Taste Standards (Client-Set)

These are your preferences. Update in `qc_preferences.md`. We calibrate to these but may push back if a preference conflicts with conversion performance.

**When preferences and performance conflict:**
We'll flag it explicitly: "Your preference is X, but our data suggests Y performs better. Your call." You decide.

---

## Revision Policy

**Included revisions per deliverable:** <!-- Set during onboarding -->
**What counts as a revision:** A change in direction, tone, or strategy (not typo fixes)
**What doesn't count:** Minor copy tweaks, formatting adjustments, typo corrections

---

## Escalation

If we disagree on quality or direction:
1. Reference the F6, does it support your position or ours?
2. If F6 is ambiguous, we discuss and update the F6
3. Client has final say on taste. Agency has final say on strategy.
